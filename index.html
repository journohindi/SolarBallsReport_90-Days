SolarBalls Hindi (@solarballs_india) - 90 Day Performance & Growth Report

Situation Now - Long Videos (Last 90 Days)

The channel currently has 875K+ subscribers with over 159M total views. In the last 90 days, the channel is running a consistent weekly release schedule but experiencing severe viewer attrition on sequential episodic content.

  • Mar 14, 2026: Suraj Tak Ka Mission - Part 1 — ~90.2K views
  • Mar 21, 2026: Suraj Tak Ka Mission - Part 2 — ~65.3K views (27% drop)
  • Mar 31, 2026: Suraj Tak Ka Mission - Part 3 — ~54.7K views (16% drop from Part 2)
  • Apr 11, 2026: Our Planets Gone Rogue Part 2 — ~45.3K views

Recent long-form videos are only activating roughly 5% to 10% of the subscriber base.

Situation Now - Shorts (Last 90 Days)

The channel has completely halted organic Shorts production. Over the last 90 days, there is zero new top-of-funnel traffic being driven by native YouTube Shorts. All circulating Shorts metrics are derived entirely from legacy uploads published over a year ago or unauthorized clips reposted by third-party channels.

Problems Found - Long Video

  • The "Part 1/Part 2" Trap: The algorithm suppresses episodic "Part 2/3" videos because they have inherently lower click-through rates (CTR). Viewers who missed Part 1 will not click Part 2. This is causing a documented 40-50% view bleed from the start to the end of a series.
  • Metadata Bloat: Titles are cluttered with dual-language phrasing (e.g., "Our Planets Gone Rogue Part 2 || Hindi || Humare Planets..."). This eats up valuable visual space on mobile devices and dilutes the core hook.
  • Dead Subscriber Weight: 875K subs generating 50K views means the algorithm assumes the content is bad because the core audience is ignoring it. The channel needs to break out of its own subscriber pool to reach new viewers.

Problems Found - Shorts

  • Abandoned Discovery Funnel: Shorts are currently YouTube's most aggressive tool for reaching non-subscribers. By abandoning Shorts, the channel has severed its primary mechanism for audience acquisition.
  • Inefficient Asset Utilization: High-retention, high-quality animation is already being rendered for long videos. Failing to cut these into 60-second vertical formats leaves guaranteed algorithmic impressions on the table.

Solutions for Long Video Problems

  • Eliminate Episodic Titles: If a story requires multiple parts, do not label them "Part 2" or "Part 3". Package every video as a standalone narrative with a unique, high-stakes hook. (Instead of "Lost Earth Part 2", title it "Earth Ki Nayi Jagah Kahan Hai?").
  • Ruthless Title Optimization: Drop the "|| Hindi ||" tags from the title. The thumbnail language and channel name already establish the language. Use the freed-up character space for emotional or high-stakes Hindi copywriting.
  • Consolidate Runtimes: Combine 7-minute multi-part episodes into single, high-retention 15-20 minute features to maximize Average View Duration (AVD) and ad revenue.

Solutions for Short Video Problems

  • Clip Extraction Pipeline: Implement a mandatory rule: Every long-form video must yield 2-3 Shorts. Extract the most chaotic, visually striking 40-second moments.
  • Cross-Linking: Use the "Related Video" feature in the YouTube Studio Shorts editor to hard-link every single Short directly to the full-length long-form video it was pulled from.
  • Vertical Re-framing: Do not just crop the 16:9 video. Ensure the central subjects (the planets) are centered in the 9:16 frame and add dynamic, fast-paced captions specifically optimized for mobile scrolling.

Overall Solutions to Grow the Channel

  • Pivot to Standalone "What If" Concepts: The channel's historical data proves that hypothetical scenarios ("What if the Sun disappeared?", "Why is Venus hot?") vastly outperform character-driven lore arcs. Focus 80% of production on high-concept educational/hypothetical setups.
  • Re-engage the Community Tab: Run polls, post lore questions, and drop high-res image teasers 48 hours prior to uploads to warm up the algorithmic cache for the 875K dormant subscribers.
  • Thumbnail A/B Testing: Utilize YouTube's native Test & Compare feature. Run two thumbnail variants for every upload—one lore-focused, one completely exaggerated and high-contrast—to scientifically determine what drives higher CTR.
